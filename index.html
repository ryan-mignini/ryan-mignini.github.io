Gunslinger

The train's been taken over. The only things that can prevent certain doom are your special forces experience and the gun in your hands... but you're all the way in the caboose! Fight your way from car to car, flipping tables and exchanging gunfire with enemy agents as you progress through the train and save the day. Presented by the Dancing Lobsters studio, Gunslinger delivers pulse-pounding action right to your web browser.

Gunslinger can be played online here.

The Adventures of Rob and Ots

It's a tough world out there, but you don't have to brave it alone! Team up with a friend through online multiplayer to run and jump your way through 8 levels of puzzle platforming fun! Both players have unique abilities that will be required to flip switches, toggle doors, and make it to the end of each level.

The Adventures of Rob and Ots can be downloaded from its Github repository.

C++ Platformer Engine

Sometimes a project is noteworthy not for its final form, but for the process used to create it. This one represents an effort to create a game engine from scratch using the power of C++ and OpenGL. Model loading, directional lighting, octree-optimized collision detection, and simple physics come together to create a simple 3D level. Help the lizard in its quest to collect metal nuts and bolts, avoiding spikeballs and jumping from platform to platform as you do so.

This project has no downloadable releases, but its code can be viewed on its Github repository.

ZipClimber

The journey has been long and hard, but you've finally reached the top of the tallest mountain in the world... now how are you going to get down? The fast way, of course! Zip-line back to base camp, dodging boulders and racking up high scores along your way. ZipClimber, created by two members of the Dancing Lobsters studio, provides fun and challenging casual entertainment for all ages.

ZipClimber can be downloaded from its Github repository.

Frogs In The Bog

Video games aren't the only thing we can make! This first release from the Dancing Lobsters studio combines solid theming with social manipulation to create an engaging tabletop experience for 3 or more players. Race across the galaxy, dodging pirates and scooping up loot in your quest to finish with as much cargo as possible! Other players' requests for help could leave you in rags or fill your space truck with riches... but can you can trust them?

Printable materials to play Frogs in the Bog can be downloaded here (Game Board and Cards), and the game's illustrated 1-page ruleset/design document can be downloaded here.

Vacuum the Vacuum

Space is full of junk, and somebody's got to clean it up! But you've been disconnected from your support line, and are now adrift in the vacuum of space... but you've got your trusty cleaning vacuum with you! Use suction and repulsion to bend Newton's laws to your advantage as you wind your way through the death-defying labyrinth of the artificial asteroid field to the safety of low-earth orbit!

Vacuum the Vacuum can be downloaded from its Github repository.

Conway's Permutations

In the mechanical complexity of algorithms lies a simple beauty, and the power of visualization can bring it to light. Rendered in full 3D through the power of three.js, Conway's Permutations allows its users to fully experience the majesty of the classic cellular automaton known as Conway's Game of Life. Watch a selection of classic patterns play out or discover your own, tinkering with the rules of the simulation itself to discover the wonders that lie within its time-tested logic!

Conway's Permutations can be viewed online here and its code is on display at its Github repository.
